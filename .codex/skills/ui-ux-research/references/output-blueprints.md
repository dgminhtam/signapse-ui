# Output Blueprints

Use one of these response shapes unless the user explicitly wants a different artifact.

## Design Direction Proposal

Use for a new screen, redesign, or pre-implementation decision.

### Context Snapshot
- product goal
- primary users
- critical tasks
- assumptions and constraints

### Research Signals
- source, date, and the pattern worth carrying forward
- note what to adapt rather than copy

### UX Findings
- key friction points or decision criteria

### Recommended Direction
- one-paragraph design thesis
- layout structure
- visual tone
- primary interactions

### Wireframe Notes
- top-level regions in reading order
- what appears above the fold
- where actions, filters, states, and secondary content should live

### Implementation Guardrails
- what must remain simple
- what should be consistent with the existing product
- what to validate during implementation

## UX Audit

Use for reviewing an existing screen or flow.

### What Works
- 2-4 strengths worth preserving

### Findings
- issue
- why it matters
- suggested change

### Priority
- now
- next
- later

### Risk Notes
- confusion risk
- data-loss risk
- accessibility risk

## Trend Scan

Use for "research trend" or "what is current" requests.

### Trend Signals
- exact source and date
- what is changing
- why teams are adopting it

### Relevance Filter
- fits this product because
- avoid or downplay because

### Direction Decision
- keep
- adapt
- skip

## Writing Rules

- Stay concise and decision-ready.
- Prefer direct recommendations over neutral summaries when the evidence is strong.
- Make trade-offs explicit.
- Match the user's language. For Signapse product surfaces, default to professional Vietnamese copy for user-facing examples.
